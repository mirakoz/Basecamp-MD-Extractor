**Josh Mings**   
Thanks, Fathy, very good info.   
  
I'd like to make a suggestion. You may be aware of this already. **Google Trends** has always been an instrumental tool in driving our previous growth, in general, and against competitors, specifically. I think, primarily, because it's based on actual search volume + specific product category, not TLD/corp/brand activity.  
  
I would look at three trends primarily (and look at both US and Worldwide over 12 months):  
  
**KeyShot vs Competitor search volume:**  
[https://trends.google.com/trends/explore?geo=US&q=keyshot,%2Fm%2F06bpry…](https://trends.google.com/trends/explore?geo=US&q=keyshot,%2Fm%2F06bpry,blender%20cycles,%2Fg%2F11ny1n2zdt,%2Fm%2F0gmdjd_&hl=en "https://trends.google.com/trends/explore?geo=US&q=keyshot,%2Fm%2F06bpry,blender%20cycles,%2Fg%2F11ny1n2zdt,%2Fm%2F0gmdjd_&hl=en")  
  
Then I would build content to compete against the closest one. This would help focus on one instead of all. We buried Maxwell and others doing this, and made headway against Octane, Redshift, Cycles, and others.  
  
**KeyShot vs. top Competitor vs Industry terms:**  
[https://trends.google.com/trends/explore?geo=US&q=keyshot,%2Fm%2F06bpry…](https://trends.google.com/trends/explore?geo=US&q=keyshot,%2Fm%2F06bpry,3d%20rendering&hl=en-US "https://trends.google.com/trends/explore?geo=US&q=keyshot,%2Fm%2F06bpry,3d%20rendering&hl=en-US")  
  
I would do everything possible to get our search volume above the industry terms, which would in turn help our rank against other competitors.  
  
**Related Industry terms:**  
[https://trends.google.com/trends/explore?geo=US&q=3d%20rendering,3D%20A…](https://trends.google.com/trends/explore?geo=US&q=3d%20rendering,3D%20AI,%2Fm%2F03qk33,%2Fm%2F02tw8p&hl=en-US "https://trends.google.com/trends/explore?geo=US&q=3d%20rendering,3D%20AI,%2Fm%2F03qk33,%2Fm%2F02tw8p&hl=en-US")  
  
This is interesting because you can gauge where interest is heading and adjust the focus accordingly.  
  
Of all the analysis and paid tools I've used over the years, this has been my go to for acting on competition quickly. A lot of this boiled down to increased social activity and content distribution - focusing on content over the competitor, really. Using the right terms of course, but using those in every piece of content generated, even down to the file names.   
  
Hope this is insightful in some way.
