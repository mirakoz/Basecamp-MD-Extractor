### Updated Monthly Media Budget Allocation | 2026

Posted by Vahag on Jan 2

[![Vahag](https://asset1.basecamp.com/2325709/people/19122740/photo/avatar.96.gif "Vahag")](/2325709/people/19122740)

Hi Fathy, Mucahit

Starting now, we need to adjust budgets to align with the following monthly spend structure across channels:

* Google: $43,500
* Bing: $5,000
* Meta: $10,000
* Pinterest: $3,450
* Reddit: $4,000
* LinkedIn: $2,000

The total approved media budget for 2026 is $815K, which averages to approximately $68K per month. Please update the budgets accordingly.

**What this means:**  
  
At this stage, we need to prioritize performance over awareness. That said, we will continue investing in awareness channels and remain flexible in reallocating budget among them based on historical performance (engagement, view-through conversions, etc.).

Feel free to share recommendations on reallocating budget across non-search channels where you see opportunities to improve performance.

**What can change:**

The door towards getting more budget for 2026 is not closed. We still can make a case for it but we need to have a few key things covered to be able to do it. Some of those are:

* Having a strong tie between budget spent and revenue generated
* Proving out the effectiveness of some of the new channels like Pinterest and Reddit
* Finding ways to convert more non EDU customers through paid channels
* Having strong non-branded campaigns that are capable of moving qualified leads down the funnel
* Having a clear messaging and creatives plan (this one is on me)

  
Happy to answer any questions at our next meeting.
