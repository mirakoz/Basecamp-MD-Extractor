Hi Luciana,  
  

Regarding the new locations and the use of multiple phone numbers on the website, Google Ads can only track calls for a single phone number at a time. If there is more than one phone number on the site, we may miss calls from users.

There are two possible solutions. The first is implementing a call tracking CRM, which would require a monthly subscription. The second option is creating separate web pages for each additional location, or ideally using a separate domain or subdomain, such as pennsylvania.trustbci.com, along with a main phone number for the header and homepage. This way, we can ensure all calls from ads are properly tracked.

Regarding the lower phone call traffic in recent days, we reviewed the ads and did not observe anything unusual. However, we increased the bid adjustment for call assets to help them appear more frequently, especially considering the lower lead quality from form submissions based on the client’s feedback.

Please let me know if you have any questions.

  
Best,  
Can

Hi Team (including Mariam), see below the analysis of leads from this client:  

I have put together an analysis of our **January lead activity** by comparing inbound calls and website form submissions against our client list to see how many leads actually converted into paying clients.

One important note before the numbers: **January was impacted by nearly two weeks of severe weather across the Charlotte region**, which likely reduced overall lead activity and slowed some scheduling. Even with that disruption, marketing still produced solid results.

  
January Lead Summary

**Total Leads (Calls + Forms): 44**

• Call Leads: 27  
• Website Form Leads: 17

**Confirmed Clients from Leads: 9**

**Overall Lead-to-Client Conversion Rate: 20.5%**

That means roughly **1 out of every 5 inbound leads became a paying client**, which is considered a strong conversion rate for investigative services.

  
Call Lead Performance

**Total Call Records:** 31  
**Unique Callers:** 27

**Clients from Calls:** 5  
**Call Conversion Rate:** 18.5%

Calls came from the following campaigns:

• Search – Private Investigator 2.0: 17 calls  
• Performance Max – Leads: 7 calls  
• Search – AI Max: 6 calls  
• Brand Search: 1 call

The **Search – Private Investigator 2.0 campaign generated the most calls and the most confirmed clients**, making it the strongest performer in January.

  
Website Form Lead Performance

**Unique Form Submissions:** 17

**Clients from Form Leads:** 4

**Form Conversion Rate:** 23.5%

So about **1 in 4 form submissions converted into a client**.

Lead Source Breakdown

Here’s where our leads came from:

**Google Ads (CPC): 12 leads**

These came from searches like:  
• private investigator  
• private investigator charlotte nc  
• professional private detective  
• private investigator near me

These searches produced many of the **highest-intent investigation inquiries**.

**Google Business Profile / Organic Search: 3 leads**

Examples include:  
• referrals from attorneys  
• direct searches for our business

**Performance Max Campaigns: 2 leads**

These tend to capture broader searches and supplemental traffic.

**Direct / Unknown Source: remaining leads**

These may include repeat visitors, referrals, or people navigating directly to the website.

  
General Lead Insights

The majority of qualified inquiries were related to:

• Infidelity/cheating spouse investigations  
• Child custody investigations  
• Skip tracing / locating individuals  
• Attorney-related investigative work

These categories are consistent with the **most common and highest-value investigative services**.

  
Overall Takeaway

Even with weather disruptions in January, marketing generated:

• **44 total inbound leads**  
• **9 confirmed clients**  
• **~20% lead conversion rate**

That indicates the current marketing channels are producing **qualified leads and real business opportunities**.
