**Mirak Ozoglu**   

Hi Francisco,

Thank you for sharing this with us, and we completely understand your concern regarding both the verification and the recent billing.

Regarding the Google Ads Advertiser Verification: Google periodically requests re-verification or additional details to ensure a safe ad ecosystem. Because the account is under the client's name and billing, it would be best if you could complete this verification by acting as the client, exactly as we successfully did back in November 2023. We kindly ask that you complete this; you can do it by logging into Google Ads, navigating to **Tools & Settings > Billing > Advertiser Verification**, and following the prompts. Let us know if you run into any roadblocks there!

Regarding the budget and billing: We hear you loud and clear, and staying strictly within your approved budget is our top priority. Sometimes Google bills an account multiple times a month because the account hits its "payment threshold" (a set amount that triggers an automatic charge), rather than waiting to charge once at the end of the month. This can make it look like the limit was exceeded.

To clarify, we will remain under the CA$1,100 limit this month as usual. Please have a look at the attached billing summary for April; we have only spent CA$706.90 this month so far.

We look forward to speaking with you and Mirella soon!

Best regards,

Mirak

**Mirak Ozoglu**   

Hi Francisco,

Absolutely no worries at all regarding the meeting mix-up! Whenever you and the client are ready to review the renewal deck, just let us or Mirella know, and we will get it scheduled.

Regarding the bot submissions: thank you for bringing this to our attention immediately. We take lead quality very seriously. Interestingly, this directly aligns with the CA$19.80 credit for "Invalid activity" that we noticed on the April billing statement. Google's automated systems caught and refunded some of this bot traffic, but it appears some submissions still managed to slip through their filters and your re-Captcha.

We are launching an immediate technical audit on our end to identify and block the source. Specifically, we are:

* Auditing the Google Search Partners and Display Network settings, as these external networks are often the primary source of sudden bot spikes.

* Double-checking our advanced location targeting settings to ensure we are strictly targeting users physically present in our target areas, rather than those using VPNs.

* Reviewing the campaign placements to exclude any suspicious IP addresses or low-quality sites.

To help us trace this perfectly: Could you ask the client to share 3 or 4 recent examples of these bot submissions (specifically the timestamp/date, and the fake name/email used)? This allows us to cross-reference the exact clicks in Google Ads and permanently block the specific IP addresses or placements they originated from.

We will get this cleaned up right away.
