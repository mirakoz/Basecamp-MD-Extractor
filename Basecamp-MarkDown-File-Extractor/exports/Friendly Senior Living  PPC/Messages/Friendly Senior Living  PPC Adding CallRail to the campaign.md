**Yigit Gok**   
Thanks for the credentials Mike. But before setting it up, we need to determine how many phone numbers we will need to create on Callrail. This is why I have been asking about what they want to track.  
  
Callrail provides up to 5 phone numbers as part of their package. Any additional phone number will cost $5/mo.  
  
In order for me to determine how many numbers we'll need to create, please communicate the following with the client and let us know;  
  

* *Does the client want CallRail to track all of their phone calls? Including the ones coming from organic, social, direct and other channels*
* *Does the client want to see the incoming calls to each of the different phone numbers on their website? (Clowerwood, Linden Knoll, Lovejoy, Glenmere, Friendly Home and number in the Footer)*

*Answers to the above will determine how many phone numbers they'll need in their Callrail subscription.*  
**Some more details to how Callrail works;**

  
So here's how it works: In order to track the phone calls, we need Callrail to swap a phone number on the website with one of its own. For example, on this page: <https://www.friendlyseniorliving.org/about/care-services/> you see a phone number 585-381-1600. Callrail changes this with one of its phone numbers to track and record the calls made to this number.   
  
However, this is only 1 phone number they have on their website. They also have phone numbers for all their 5 locations/communities. This gives us a total of 6 different phone numbers to track.   
  
Now, there are 2 approaches here;  
1- We can use only 1 Callrail number to replace all of their 6 numbers. If we do that, we can track all of the numbers but we won't be able to determine which phone number works better or is more qualified.  
2- We can use 6 Callrail numbers. 1 for each phone number on the website. This will allow us to see the performances of each of their different phone numbers.  
  
  
On the other hand, If the client wants us to track all of their channels (Organic, Direct, Social, Referral, etc.) We will need 1 to 6 more Callrail phone numbers depending on their approach above. If they want to see individual phone number/location/community performances for each channels, they will need 6 numbers per channel. If they are okay with not being able to see individual phone number/location/community and okay with seeing general channel performances, 1 Callrail number per channel should be enough.   
  
This is important that we get a proper answer and a confirmation from the client as it will change the Callrail fees.  
  
Depending on their answers, we may either need 1 Callrail number or +30.  
  
All the best,  
Yigit
